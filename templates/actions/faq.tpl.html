  • Can you help me find a lot or a tear-down home to build a new house?

  • How does financing work?
    When it comes to financing, building on your own lot can save you a significant amount of money. If you are tearing down your existing home, you can use the equity you have built up to avoid putting a down payment which ultimately leads to reducing your interest rate. Even if you are buying a lot or tear-down on which to build, the savings from building on your lot means that you may be able to reduce your down payment and perhaps even your interest rate. The loan you would receive is called a Construction-to-Permanent loan. It is very similar to a mortgage except that instead of paying all at once, it pays out in installments as the home is constructed. When the project is complete, the loan becomes a normal end loan similar to a mortgage. The rates and terms are the same as what is available for mortgages except that you have more flexibility to do things; for example, rolling in payments during construction and adding allowances for other items such as adding a deck or landscaping.

  • If I want to make changes to any of the floor plans or designs, what are the costs involved?
    Usually there are architectural or design fees associated with any significant changes to the original plans or designs due to new drawings required. The amount can vary; however, it is usually between $1500 to $5000 depending on the amount and type of changes.
  • What other costs should I be aware of if I decide to build on my own lot?
    You need to consider site work (any work that must be done on your lot such as demolition, connecting to utilities, site plans, landscaping, and etc) and county fees. These costs vary depending on lot conditions and county regulations. We can assist you in determining an approximate range of costs.

We receive no kickbacks or commissions from the contractors we refer you to for completing your site work. We know this may be your first time ever building a home; therefore, we want this to be a smooth process for you. Don't worry - we're there with you all the way!
