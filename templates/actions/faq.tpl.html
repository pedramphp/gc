We understand the real costs of building new homes inside and out, so we’ll do what we can to relieve some of the financial burden on you by being as cost effective as possible. A fair amount of custom builders, however, will charge you more and call it “variance costs”, saying that it will be used in the event that something unexpected arises during construction. We cut out this part so you will be able to save more. We’ve built a reputation in the building industry for our construction quality, homeowner satisfaction, and overall value for the dollar that are unmatched to this day.

  • Can you help me find a lot or a tear-down home to build a new house?

  • How does financing work?
    Building on your own lot can save you a fortune in the long run. You can reduce your interest rate by tearing down your existing home on the lot and putting the equity towards a down payment. During the project, you will receive a Construction-to-Permanent loan that works much like a mortgage, but it pays out in installments as the home is built instead of all at once and by the end of the project the loan is not much more different from a mortgage. While the loan may have the same rates and terms as a mortgage, there are also certain benefits you receive during the course of the project: rolling payments during construction, adding allowances to allow for additions such as adding a patio or proper landscaping and much more.
  • Will you build from my own plans?
    We will review your plans and determine whether we are the right builder for you. Prices may be higher for completely custom homes, but this is not always the case. We would love to be given the opportunity to build your dream home, whether it is one of our models or your own. Usually there are no extra fees associated with bringing your own plans.
  • If I want to make changes to any of the floor plans or designs, what are the costs involved?
    Usually there are architectural or design fees associated with any significant changes to the original plans or designs due to new drawings required. The amount can vary; however, it is usually between $1500 to $5000 depending on the amount and type of changes.
  • What other costs should I be aware of if I decide to build on my own lot?
    It is important that you are aware of additional site work (such as demolition, utility connection, site plans, landscaping, and etc) and county fees that your lot may need. These costs vary depending on lot conditions and county regulations, but we will be able to assist you in these matters as well.
  • How do I go about completing my site work? Do you assist with this?
    We pride ourselves in being involved from prior to the start of construction all the way to the end. We will assist you in managing your site work without charging you any management fees as well as allowing you to pick your own contractors and pay them directly. And don’t worry; we don’t get any sort of compensation or kickback for referring you to our trusted contractors. Whether this is you first home being built or not, we will do whatever we can to make the process go as smoothly as possible: you can count on us!
